# 09 — Checklist-Driven Refinements To The Prompt Waves

## Why the prompt waves were refined
The attached checklist and scorecard add two important controls that the first-pass audit implied but did not formalize enough:

1. **scored acceptance discipline**
2. **traceable closure criteria**

The implementation waves must therefore do more than “improve Safety.”
They must now prove that each wave removes specific checklist failures and improves the scorecard without hiding unresolved hard-stop defects.

## New prompt-package requirements
Every wave now carries the following additional expectations:

- use the checklist as an execution-time audit tool, not just a retrospective summary
- report which checklist areas were materially improved by the wave
- report which checklist areas remain below acceptable threshold after the wave
- do not claim closure for any wave if it still leaves behind a new hard-stop failure in the touched scope
- final closure must include an updated scorecard and explicit acceptance call

## Wave-specific checklist emphasis

### Wave 01 — shell posture, contract depth, application-mode truth
Primary checklist categories:
- Doctrine and host compliance
- Purpose-fit sophistication and persona expression
- Homepage integration quality
- Breakpoint and shell-fit quality
- Contract, data, and backend seam rigor

Expected score movement:
- raise shell/homepage posture out of obvious failure territory
- define explicit app-mode behavior instead of relying on shell optimism
- establish enough contract depth to support the rebuild

### Wave 02 — rebuilt surface + consumer integration
Primary checklist categories:
- UI-kit / premium-stack compliance
- Token and styling discipline
- Surface composition and hierarchy
- Interaction completeness
- Identity, media, and attribution quality
- State-model completeness

Expected score movement:
- replace the generic-card-adjacent outcome
- materially strengthen hierarchy and persona expression
- produce a more complete, productized user journey

### Wave 03 — proof and real closure
Primary checklist categories:
- Accessibility and keyboard behavior
- Host-runtime resilience
- Validation and closure proof

Expected score movement:
- convert assumptions into evidence
- prove hosted/package truth
- produce a credible final acceptance decision against the scorecard

## Final closure gate
Do not close the overall effort until:
- the rebuilt Safety effort reaches **40+/56** at minimum
- no category relevant to the touched scope remains below **2** without an explicit written exception
- no hard-stop failure remains open
- the final closure note explicitly states whether the result is merely homepage-grade or genuinely benchmark-grade
