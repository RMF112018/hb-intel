# 06 — Gap Register

| ID | Gap | Severity | Current impact | Required outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| G-01 | Contrast is not reliable across the image/crop set | Critical | Greeting and logo legibility vary too much by crop and brightness | Measured, repeatable text-safe and logo-safe contrast across approved images and breakpoints |
| G-02 | Background cropping is governed only by generic center-crop behavior | Critical | Text and logo placement depend too heavily on luck | Per-image focal metadata or equivalent crop-safe strategy |
| G-03 | Hero fallback treatment drifts from homepage overlay doctrine | High | No-image state still reads too close to a blue/orange gradient-wash answer | Deep-charcoal fallback with restrained warmth and grain, no branded wash behavior |
| G-04 | Logo scale is not breakpoint-native enough | High | Logo becomes too assertive in tighter states | Responsive lockup rules tuned by real composition mode, not only simple clamp ranges |
| G-05 | Top band lacks enough visual continuity between hero and launcher | High | Entry stack still feels segmented | Hero + actions should read as one authored flagship zone |
| G-06 | Tablet and handheld hero modes are still compressed desktop thinking | High | Mobile feels serviceable, not premium | Handheld and tablet-specific composition rules that reduce and re-anchor content intentionally |
| G-07 | Runtime proof is not closure-grade | High | Diagnostics exist but acceptance evidence is weak | Hosted screenshot matrix, contrast proof, breakpoint validation, and packaging proof |
| G-08 | Styling is not governed tightly enough for long-term flagship iteration | Medium | Too many one-off visual decisions in CSS | Introduce clearer hero variant / mode structure and stronger tokenized or governed local primitives |
| G-09 | Brand lockup doctrine may not be fully satisfied | Medium | Current visible mark emphasizes company logo more than explicit HB Central lockup | Confirm or revise lockup asset/treatment to match doctrine |
