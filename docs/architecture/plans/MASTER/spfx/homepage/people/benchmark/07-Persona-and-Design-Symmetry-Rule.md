# 07 — Persona and Design Symmetry Rule

## Purpose

This addendum exists to remove any ambiguity:

**The homepage benchmark package must never cause all homepage webparts to converge into the same product surface with different labels.**

## Governing interpretation

HB Kudos Public is the benchmark for:
- quality
- rigor
- maturity
- completeness
- host-aware execution
- design-system seriousness

It is **not** the mandatory visual template for all homepage work.

## Required balance

Every homepage webpart must achieve both of the following at the same time:

### 1. Brand and design symmetry
The webpart must feel like it belongs to the same HB homepage family.

### 2. Persona-fit differentiation
The webpart must express a tone, information architecture, and interaction posture appropriate to its own content and mission.

## What must stay common

Across `apps/hb-webparts`, the following should remain recognizably related:

- overall brand posture
- premium quality level
- primitive discipline
- spacing/hierarchy seriousness
- accessibility quality
- host-aware execution
- closure rigor

## What should differ when appropriate

The following may and often should differ between homepage webparts:

- emotional tone
- content density
- interaction rhythm
- card/formality level
- detail depth
- editorial versus operational posture
- motion intensity
- scan behavior
- information framing

## Examples

### HB Kudos
May be:
- warm
- engaging
- fun
- human
- celebratory

### Project Spotlight
May be:
- professional
- informative
- confidence-building
- clear
- executive-friendly

### Company Pulse
May be:
- editorial
- timely
- headline-driven
- feed-oriented
- newsroom-like

### Tool Launcher / Work Hub
May be:
- operational
- compact
- efficient
- command-oriented
- utility-first

## Failure conditions

The benchmark package has failed if it encourages outcomes where:

- every homepage webpart uses the same hero logic
- every homepage webpart uses the same card recipe
- every homepage webpart uses the same emotional tone
- the target webpart loses its content-fit persona
- “premium” becomes a synonym for “copy the Kudos public surface”

## Enforcement rule

Every homepage audit, implementation package, and closure review must ask:

1. Is this webpart benchmark-grade?
2. Does it comply with the doctrine?
3. Does it preserve HB family symmetry?
4. Does it still feel like the correct webpart for its content?

If the answer to #4 is no, the work is not complete.
