# HB Kudos Wave 3 — Audit Summary

## Wave 3 findings addressed by this package

### 15. The public experience is compositionally fragmented
The public HB Kudos experience spans several adjacent surfaces and interaction states that do not yet feel fully harmonized as one product system.

### 16. “View all”, article reading, archive, and composer shells are not yet harmonized as one design language
The shared flyout shell exists, but the internal content grammar, body rhythm, metadata behavior, and panel language are still not unified enough.

### 17. There is evidence of accessibility intent, but it is not systematic enough
Focus-visible and reduced-motion handling exist in places, but the treatment is still ad hoc rather than clearly systemized across the experience.

### 18. Companion controls are semantically serviceable, but visually and behaviorally fragmented
The controls generally function, but the workspace still needs stronger consistency and clarity in control rhythm and interaction language.

### 19. The “Read more” and flyout-body patterns need stronger content accessibility discipline
These patterns work today, but they need stronger structure, semantics, and consistency so they are more robust and easier to use.

### 20. Host-safe behavior is real, but the implementation is brittle
The intent is correct, especially around assistant overlap and hosted conditions, but the implementation should be more reliable and easier to reason about.

## Wave 3 remediation posture

This wave should tighten the user-facing experience without destabilizing the product model.
It is a cohesion, clarity, accessibility, and host-hardening wave.
It is not a broad architecture rewrite and not the final production-readiness closure wave.
