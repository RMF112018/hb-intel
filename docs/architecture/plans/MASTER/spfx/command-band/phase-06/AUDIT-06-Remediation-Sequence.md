# 06 — Remediation Sequence

## Sequence overview

### Step 1 — Lock architecture boundary and flagship contract
Why first:
- it prevents the redesign from solving the problem by breaking the ownership model
- it creates an explicit flagship target for the shared surface family

### Step 2 — Rebuild the flagship shared surface
Why second:
- the card-like surface is the single largest contributor to the hosted underperformance

### Step 3 — Harden overflow interaction and accessibility
Why third:
- overflow is a core part of the command-band identity and should not be treated as a minor follow-up

### Step 4 — Recalibrate wrapper envelope and transition quality
Why fourth:
- once the rail surface is stronger, wrapper spacing can be tuned precisely around the real new result

### Step 5 — Harden breakpoint presentation and regression coverage
Why fifth:
- after visual and compositional changes, budgets and normalization paths need proof

### Step 6 — Refresh docs and closure artifacts
Why sixth:
- closure documents should reflect actual code and proof, not the other way around

### Step 7 — Run hosted validation
Why last:
- this is the binding closure gate
