# 02C — HB Homepage Brand Foundation Reference

## Purpose

Provide a concise, implementation-ready brand reference for homepage engineers and site owners building on Prompt-02 contracts.

## Locked Homepage Brand Inputs

- Primary blue: `rgb(34, 83, 145)` (`#225391`)
- Secondary orange: `rgb(229, 126, 70)` (`#E57E46`)
- Tone: premium, established, polished, operational
- Anti-tone: not flashy, not template-like, not startup-like

## Component-Level Brand Rules

### Hero typography

- Use strong but disciplined headline typography.
- Prioritize legibility and hierarchy over decorative styling.

### Section headers

- Maintain consistent structure and spacing so zones read as one coordinated system.
- Avoid ad hoc style drift between editorial, utility, and operational sections.

### Editorial cards

- Support authored hierarchy (featured vs secondary).
- Preserve calm information density and readable contrast.

### Utility tiles

- Optimize clarity and quick recognition of intent.
- Avoid dashboard-like control density in homepage contexts.

### Intelligence strips / spotlight cards

- Prioritize signal readability and concise status expression.
- Keep treatment lightweight and homepage-appropriate.

## Interaction and State Rules

- Light-theme-first is mandatory.
- Focus, hover, disabled, and loading states must remain explicit and readable.
- Motion must be tasteful and must respect reduced-motion preferences.

## Prompt-02 Closure Check

- Brand values are encoded as concrete implementation references.
- Component-level visual expectations are explicit for Prompts 03–10.
- Brand guidance now complements (and does not replace) broader `ui-kit` governance.
