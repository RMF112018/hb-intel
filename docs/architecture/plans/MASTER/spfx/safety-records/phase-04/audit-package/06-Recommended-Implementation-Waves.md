# Recommended Implementation Waves

## Wave 1 — Structural product-surface uplift

### Objective
Move the Safety app out of the “governed enterprise workflow” tier and into a visibly productized, doctrine-compliant Safety surface.

### Scope
1. Define the application breakpoint / narrowest-stable contract.
2. Rebuild the Upload page into a flagship intake experience.
3. Strengthen the authored Safety surface language.
4. Recompose the Reporting Period and Review surfaces so they are not dominated by plain filter/table composition.

### Why first
These are the highest-leverage changes. Without them, the app will still fail on:
- generic dominant posture
- breakpoint quality
- first-impression credibility
- flagship identity

### Out of scope
- broad backend redesign
- unrelated homepage shell work
- speculative feature expansion beyond the current route model

---

## Wave 2 — Trust, depth, and closure

### Objective
Turn the strongest existing surfaces into benchmark-grade detail and evidence experiences, then close proof-quality acceptance.

### Scope
1. Upgrade inspection and project-week detail composition.
2. Improve provenance, authority, and source-workbook treatment.
3. Close accessibility and keyboard/touch gaps.
4. Capture SharePoint-hosted proof across the required breakpoint matrix.

### Why second
The current detail pages are already the strongest part of the app. They are better upgraded after the shared product language and responsive contract are established.

### Exit criteria
- no dominant generic enterprise posture
- explicit responsive contract implemented
- upload first-run experience feels flagship
- review/period surfaces read as operational command surfaces
- hosted screenshots and package/host proof captured
