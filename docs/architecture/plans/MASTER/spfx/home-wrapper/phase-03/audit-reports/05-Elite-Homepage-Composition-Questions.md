# Elite Homepage Composition Questions

These are the questions strong enterprise audits usually miss, but elite homepage reviews ask immediately.

## 1. What is the homepage actually trying to do first?
Not “what modules are available?”
Not “what was already built?”

The real question is:
**What is the first job of this homepage in the first 5 seconds?**

Applied here:
- The homepage should immediately establish brand, top tasks, and project/work relevance.
- That means the first shell lane should feel operationally valuable, not merely sequentially correct.

## 2. Which surface earns the right to dominate?
Elite homepages do not let every module speak at the same volume.

Applied here:
- Project Portfolio Spotlight and Company Pulse have the strongest claim to first-lane dominance.
- Leadership, People & Culture, and Kudos should not compete for the same first impression.

## 3. What disappears when the screen gets tighter?
Great responsive homepages do not merely shrink.
They **decide**.

Applied here:
- the utility band should hide lower-priority actions
- the shell should stack intentionally
- some modules should demote, not just compress

## 4. Is the page using width to create value or just to spread things out?
Wide screens can produce either:
- premium authored composition
- or expensive emptiness

Applied here:
- the shell’s current ultrawide policy is stronger in principle than in realized layout
- wide-state composition must be made real, not theoretical

## 5. Does the first fold prove the page is useful?
An elite homepage gives evidence of utility immediately.

Applied here:
- the shell should not make the user scroll past branding and polite context before seeing operational value
- the first shell lane must feel like proof, not promise

## 6. Which modules become stronger when paired, and which become weaker?
Side-by-side layout is not inherently better.
Some pairs amplify each other; others dilute each other.

Applied here:
- Project Portfolio Spotlight + Company Pulse is a strong pair
- People & Culture + Kudos is a weak pair and is correctly restricted
- Leadership + Project Spotlight is weaker than Spotlight + Company Pulse

## 7. What is system-authored, and what is configurable?
Elite surfaces do not turn into freeform dashboards too early.

Applied here:
- hero identity
- first-fold value rules
- pairing prohibitions
- recognition ceilings
- mobile single-column rules

should remain system-authored.

Preset selection, optional visibility, and limited reorderability can become configurable later.

## 8. Is the page memorable because of structure, not decoration?
Weak pages rely on styling.
Strong pages rely on hierarchy and sequencing.

Applied here:
- the next win is not “more polish”
- it is stronger shell authorship

## 9. Is there one outlier that drags the page down?
World-class pages are often limited by the weakest important module, not the strongest one.

Applied here:
- the outlier is People & Culture Public
- until it reaches the same governance rigor, the homepage will feel uneven

## 10. Can this page grow without becoming a parking lot?
This is one of the most important elite-review questions.

Applied here:
- the shell is finally close to having the right architecture for growth
- but growth will still degrade unless preset grammar, occupant ceilings, and configuration governance are strengthened now

## 11. What does the page refuse to do?
Strong homepages have boundaries.

Applied here:
The homepage should refuse to:
- feature everything equally
- expose every action all the time
- let recognition dominate the top of the page
- let future maintainer controls become a freeform canvas

## 12. Which decisions are old history rather than current truth?
Elite reviews are ruthless about removing legacy sequencing assumptions.

Applied here:
- Leadership-before-Spotlight is a legacy sequencing habit, not the strongest current answer
- single-column full-width bands for everything are no longer justified as the default flagship model

## Net result when these questions are applied

The homepage should evolve toward:
- a stronger operational/editorial first lane
- clearer contextual demotion of secondary modules
- a real wide-state composition
- and a tightly governed future configuration system
