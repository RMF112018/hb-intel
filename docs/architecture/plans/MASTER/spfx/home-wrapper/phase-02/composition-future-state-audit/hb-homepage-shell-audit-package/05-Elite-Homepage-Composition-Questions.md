# Elite Homepage Composition Questions

## Why this section matters
Ordinary enterprise audits ask:
- does it work?
- is it responsive?
- does it look clean?

Elite audits ask better questions:
- what wins attention first, and should it?
- what deserves dominance versus mere presence?
- what is silently competing?
- what cognitive work is the page asking of users?
- what becomes easier at each width, and what becomes harder?
- what should remain fixed because it protects quality?

## The most important elite questions

### 1. What is the page trying to make obvious in the first five seconds?
If a homepage cannot answer this clearly, its composition is failing.

**Applied here:**  
The current shell does not yet answer this strongly enough because it begins with a sequence of good modules rather than an orchestrated homepage statement.

### 2. Which modules are earning prominence, and which are merely inheriting it?
A premium homepage does not allow placement order alone to create authority.

**Applied here:**  
Current zone order gives prominence by sequence more than by authored shell strategy.

### 3. Which surfaces are competing for the same kind of attention?
World-class compositions reduce destructive competition.

**Applied here:**  
Company Pulse and Project Spotlight are the clearest collision pair.

### 4. What can be discovered later without being fully exposed now?
Elite pages do not fully expand every layer of value at once.

**Applied here:**  
Archive content, secondary rails, supporting culture rows, and some recognition depth should move toward stronger progressive disclosure patterns.

### 5. What is the calmest possible version of this page that still feels rich?
Premium pages are not measured by how much they can show, but by how much they can reveal without noise.

**Applied here:**  
The shell should shift from sequential abundance to selective emphasis.

### 6. What happens to hierarchy when the canvas narrows?
Weak pages simply stack.
Strong pages reinterpret hierarchy.

**Applied here:**  
The shell currently stacks. It does not yet reinterpret.

### 7. Which layout choices must remain system-authored to protect quality?
The best configurable systems do not expose everything.

**Applied here:**  
The future control panel should not be allowed to break dominance rules, shell rhythm, or minimum comfort widths.

### 8. Which modules are shape-compatible, not just topic-compatible?
Two modules may both belong on a homepage but still be poor neighbors.

**Applied here:**  
Leadership + People & Culture are more shape-compatible than Company Pulse + Project Spotlight in many contexts.

### 9. Which sections justify revisitation?
Elite homepages build return behavior.

**Applied here:**  
Company Pulse, Project Spotlight, HB Kudos, and future Safety surfaces justify repeat visitation more strongly than the current People & Culture surface.

### 10. What is the page’s narrative arc?
Premium compositions are authored experiences, not inventory dumps.

**Applied here:**  
The current shell lacks a strong narrative arc between editorial, operational, human, and recognition content.

### 11. Which surfaces should be context-driven rather than always visible?
Contextual reveal often produces a cleaner flagship page.

**Applied here:**  
Some future modules should likely be rotatable, context-driven, or lane-specific rather than permanently full exposure.

### 12. What is the maintenance model behind the page?
World-class pages are sustainable, not just visually strong on day one.

**Applied here:**  
The shell currently lacks the governed layout contracts needed for sustainable controlled evolution.

## The standards that separate competent from world-class

### Competent homepage
- responsive enough
- clean enough
- has good modules
- avoids obvious clutter

### World-class homepage
- establishes immediate hierarchy
- uses authored emphasis, not accidental emphasis
- makes scanning easy without flattening meaning
- knows what to defer
- transforms intelligently across widths
- protects quality when made configurable
- preserves identity while scaling to future growth
- feels intentional, not assembled

## Which of those standards are already present
Already present:
- strong child-module quality in key zones
- real shared-surface maturity
- host-aware discipline
- some purposeful responsive behavior inside modules
- recognition of premium benchmark posture

## Which are still missing
Still missing:
- shell narrative arc
- shell-owned hierarchy model
- shell-owned breakpoint reinterpretation
- governed configurability model
- equal maturity across all intended shell modules
- compositional protection against destructive prominence collisions

## Bottom line
The homepage is closer to “competent with several premium modules” than to “world-class flagship shell.”

That is a meaningful achievement.
It is not yet the target state defined in the brief.
