# Hedrick Brothers SharePoint Homepage Design Brief

## Document Purpose
This brief defines the vision, experience ambition, design direction, functional expectations, and product principles for a custom Hedrick Brothers SharePoint homepage. It is intended to guide strategy, UX design, visual design, information architecture, webpart planning, development, governance, and stakeholder alignment.

This homepage should not be treated as a standard SharePoint page with upgraded colors or nicer tiles. It should function as a premium, custom, branded company operating experience that feels meaningfully more refined, useful, and distinctive than conventional SharePoint homepage implementations.

---

## 1. Executive Summary
Hedrick Brothers needs a SharePoint homepage that reflects the company with the same level of confidence, quality, and care that it brings to its work in the field. The homepage should feel custom-built, modern, polished, and highly functional. It should combine strong aesthetics with real daily utility.

The experience should act as:
- the digital front door to the company
- the central entry point for internal communication and operational awareness
- the launch point for common tools, team spaces, and work systems
- a premium expression of the HB brand inside Microsoft 365

This is not a “better intranet page.” It is a branded internal product experience hosted on SharePoint.

---

## 2. Vision Statement
The Hedrick Brothers SharePoint homepage should be a distinctive, premium, and highly usable company homepage that reinforces brand identity, improves employee orientation, accelerates access to work, and creates a stronger sense of cohesion across the organization.

It should feel intentional and elevated from the first second a user lands on it. It should be visually impressive without being decorative, highly functional without feeling dense, and personalized without becoming casual or gimmicky.

---

## 3. Vision in One Sentence
Create a premium, custom SharePoint homepage that communicates who Hedrick Brothers is, what matters right now, and where each employee should go next—within an experience that feels elegant, modern, and unmistakably HB.

---

## 4. Strategic Objective
The homepage should establish a new internal standard for digital experience quality at Hedrick Brothers.

It should:
- break out of the look and feel of standard SharePoint webparts
- outperform default Microsoft homepage patterns in aesthetics and usability
- exceed the feel of template-driven intranet products by delivering a more bespoke and brand-specific experience
- create a reusable foundation for a broader family of branded SharePoint experiences and custom webparts

---

## 5. Brand Translation
The homepage should translate Hedrick Brothers’ public-facing identity into an internal environment.

### Brand signals to carry forward
- Uncompromised Excellence
- premium quality and professionalism
- commercial rigor paired with luxury-level presentation
- a strong Florida identity and regional presence
- breadth across commercial, luxury residential, development, and environmental work
- trust, transparency, long-term relationships, and operational discipline
- visible emphasis on safety, teamwork, integrity, growth, and execution

### What that means digitally
The homepage should feel:
- established, not startup-like
- polished, not template-like
- premium, not flashy
- human, not cold
- operational, not merely informational
- confident, not overdesigned

---

## 6. Experience Ambition
The homepage should feel closer to a custom enterprise product or premium digital front door than to a typical intranet landing page.

### It should be
- custom and differentiated
- visually memorable
- editorial in hierarchy
- operational in usefulness
- modular in construction
- curated rather than cluttered
- role-aware where it adds value
- fast to understand and fast to use

### It should not be
- a wall of equal-weight webparts
- a Microsoft-themed page with light customization
- a dumping ground for links and announcements
- a news page masquerading as a homepage
- an over-animated design concept with poor practicality
- a generic “modern workplace” template

---

## 7. Core Jobs the Homepage Must Perform
The homepage should perform six primary jobs.

### 1. Welcome
Acknowledge the employee immediately and make the first screen feel warm, deliberate, and custom.

### 2. Orient
Help the employee understand what is happening across the company and what deserves attention now.

### 3. Direct
Route the employee quickly to the right tools, systems, sites, documents, and actions.

### 4. Inform
Surface meaningful company, project, safety, and people updates in a highly digestible format.

### 5. Represent
Act as a premium internal expression of the Hedrick Brothers brand.

### 6. Create Continuity
Connect leadership communication, company culture, operations, and work systems into one coherent experience.

---

## 8. Signature Personalization Requirement
A custom greeting must be incorporated into the homepage experience as a signature element.

### Required greeting format
**Good {morning / afternoon / evening}, {First Name}.**

Examples:
- Good morning, Bobby.
- Good afternoon, Bobby.
- Good evening, Bobby.

### Purpose of the greeting
The greeting should immediately establish that:
- the homepage knows who the user is
- the experience is custom-built, not generic
- the tone is welcoming and polished
- the homepage is meant for the employee, not just posted at them

### Product intent
This should not behave like a small utility label. It should be treated as a defining part of the hero experience and first impression.

### Design expectations
- elegant, simple, and highly legible
- prominent in the hero region
- warm and professional
- not oversized, playful, or gimmicky
- not buried below the fold
- not competing with urgent alerts or critical actions

### Data and behavior expectations
- first name should come from the signed-in Microsoft 365 identity or preferred profile source
- time-of-day logic should update properly based on the user’s current local time context
- fallback behavior should exist if a preferred first name is unavailable
- personalization should not require employees to manually maintain a custom profile field if avoidable

### Supporting subtext options
The greeting may be paired with a concise supporting line such as:
- Here’s what’s happening across Hedrick Brothers today.
- Your tools, updates, and priorities are ready.
- Start with what matters most.

## 9. Target Audiences
The homepage must serve multiple audiences without becoming fragmented or diluted.

### Primary audiences
- office staff
- project executives
- project managers
- field-adjacent operations staff
- department leaders
- new hires
- support departments

### Secondary audiences
- executive leadership
- HR and people/culture stakeholders
- IT and platform owners
- occasional or infrequent users needing wayfinding

### Experience implication
The page should have a shared brand and structural backbone while allowing selective personalization, prioritization, and content targeting where appropriate.

---

## 10. Experience Principles
### 1. Premium first impression
The first screen should immediately communicate design quality, confidence, and intention.

### 2. Personal, not generic
The homepage should feel aware of the employee. The personalized greeting is a core part of this.

### 3. Useful at a glance
The page should quickly answer:
- What is happening?
- What matters most?
- Where do I go?
- What changed?
- What should I open first?

### 4. Strong hierarchy
Not all content should compete equally. The page should guide the eye clearly.

### 5. Fewer, better components
A smaller number of high-quality, highly useful webparts is preferable to many average ones.

### 6. Operationally grounded
The page should support actual work, not just communication.

### 7. Brand-consistent but not marketing-heavy
The page should reflect HB’s character without feeling like an external sales website.

### 8. Motion with discipline
Animation and transitions should add polish and clarity, not distraction.

### 9. SharePoint underneath, not all over the surface
The platform can be SharePoint, but the experience should not look constrained by default SharePoint patterns.

### 10. Scalable by design
The homepage should establish reusable visual, structural, and technical patterns that can extend across future SharePoint surfaces.

---

- confident

- not overly corporate
- not casual for the sake of being casual

## 12. Homepage Content Model
The homepage should be organized into a set of intentional zones with clear hierarchy.

### A. Hero / Top Band
Purpose: establish identity, personalization, and immediate orientation.

Should include:
- personalized greeting
- concise supporting line or daily orientation statement
- leadership or company message area
- optional high-priority alert region
- visual or editorial treatment that sets the tone for the page

### B. Priority Actions Zone
Purpose: accelerate access to the most important user-specific destinations and actions.

Should include:
- personalized shortcuts
- my tools
- my sites
- my approvals
- time-sensitive actions
- role-aware utility links

### C. Company Pulse Zone
Purpose: surface curated internal awareness.

Should include:
- key news
- project wins and milestones
- recognition moments
- safety highlights
- culture and people updates

### D. Operational Intelligence Zone
Purpose: connect the homepage to the business, not just internal communications.

Should include:
- important project or portfolio signals
- initiatives or strategic programs
- deadlines or key dates
- spotlight content tied to active operations
- relevant dashboards or summarized insights where appropriate

### E. Discovery / Wayfinding Zone
Purpose: help employees find information and destinations quickly.

Should include:
- directory and people discovery
- smart search entry point
- policy or form access
- featured teams and department spaces
- learning resources or quick-reference materials

## 14. Visual Design Direction
### Overall look and feel
The visual direction should be:
- clean
- premium
- confident
- structured
- highly legible
- editorial rather than template-driven
- modern without feeling trendy or fragile

### Visual character
The homepage should combine:
- strong typography
- generous spacing where it improves clarity
- tighter utility density where it improves workflow
- refined contrast and layering
- well-resolved card and panel systems
- subtle depth and motion
- selective use of imagery rather than decorative overload

### What to avoid visually
- default SharePoint block layouts with modest restyling
- excessive color noise
- too many card styles competing at once
- low-contrast luxury styling that hurts readability
- design ideas that look impressive in static mockups but degrade usability

---

## 15. Layout and Composition Direction
### Layout principles
- clear top-to-bottom visual hierarchy
- intentional asymmetry where it improves sophistication
- strong grid discipline
- a hero region that feels authored, not assembled
- flexible content bands that can adapt without visual collapse
- utility zones that are compact and efficient
- curated balance between large-featured content and compact actionable content

### Composition goal
The user should be able to understand the structure of the homepage within seconds.

It should feel composed, not accumulated.

---

## 16. Motion and Interaction Direction
Motion should be used to create refinement, responsiveness, and clarity.

### Appropriate motion uses
- subtle hover feedback
- polished transitions between states
- elegant loading and skeleton states
- measured content rotation or spotlight transitions
- lightweight microinteractions for tool launchers and card emphasis

### Motion guardrails
- no distracting entrance animations
- no decorative motion without purpose
- no interactions that slow down daily use
- no overreliance on carousels for critical content
- no gimmicky treatment of the greeting or welcome experience

---

## 17. Functional Expectations
The homepage must be operationally useful, not merely visually strong.

### Required qualities
- fast load perception
- resilient rendering
- strong accessibility and readability
- modular webpart architecture
- reusable component system
- responsive behavior within SharePoint constraints
- good performance on common corporate devices
- role-aware or audience-aware targeting where useful
- strong content governance and maintainability

### Additional expectations
- webparts should be configurable without code where practical
- content ownership should be clear
- governance should prevent homepage sprawl
- the experience should scale into future site surfaces and related webparts

---

## 18. Personalization Strategy
The homepage should be personalized enough to feel relevant, but not so personalized that it becomes inconsistent or difficult to govern.

### Personalization should influence
- greeting
- quick actions
- role-relevant tools
- selected content prioritization
- possibly featured sites or work destinations

### Personalization should not undermine
- brand consistency
- overall information architecture
- homepage predictability
- content governance
- executive visibility of important company-wide items

---

## 19. Content Governance Principles
The homepage must be curated and protected from sprawl.

### Governance principles
- homepage space is limited and valuable
- not every department should receive equal visual priority by default
- featured content should follow a defined hierarchy
- ownership for each zone and webpart should be assigned
- stale content should expire or rotate out
- content templates and rules should support consistency
- custom design quality should not be eroded by uncontrolled publishing patterns

---

## 20. Accessibility and Readability Expectations
A premium homepage must still be highly usable.

### Accessibility expectations
- strong color contrast
- keyboard accessibility
- semantic structure
- clear focus states
- legible typography at real usage sizes
- no essential information hidden behind hover-only behavior
- animation respectful of motion sensitivity

### Readability expectations
- concise copy
- scannable structures
- strong heading hierarchy
- no long, dense text blocks on the homepage
- balanced use of prominent and secondary content

---

## 21. Success Criteria
The homepage is successful if:
- employees immediately recognize it as a custom Hedrick Brothers experience
- the greeting becomes a recognizable signature element of the experience
- leadership feels the page represents the company at a premium level
- users can get to key tools, sites, and destinations faster than today
- important updates are more visible and better curated
- the page becomes a real starting point for the workday rather than a page users bypass
- the component system can support future homepage evolution and related SharePoint surfaces

---

## 22. Design Guardrails
### Do not
- recreate standard SharePoint with nicer styling only
- overpopulate the homepage
- allow every stakeholder request to become a homepage feature
- treat the greeting as a trivial utility detail
- lean on generic quick links as the main interaction model
- prioritize novelty over usability
- use visual richness that compromises performance or accessibility
- let content governance become an afterthought
- design a static page that cannot grow into a system

### Always
- protect hierarchy
- protect polish
- protect usefulness
- protect maintainability
- protect the brand standard

---

## 23. Strategic Outcome
The homepage should establish the design, product, and component standards for a broader branded SharePoint ecosystem at Hedrick Brothers.

It should demonstrate that SharePoint can host a serious, high-quality company experience when paired with the right design system, technical architecture, webpart strategy, and governance approach.

This homepage is not only a page. It is the first visible proof point of a larger internal digital experience standard.

## 25. Final Design Intent
The Hedrick Brothers SharePoint homepage should feel like a premium internal destination that employees want to use, leadership is proud to show, and the company can build upon.

It should be warm but disciplined, elegant but useful, custom but maintainable, and unmistakably Hedrick Brothers from the moment it loads.
